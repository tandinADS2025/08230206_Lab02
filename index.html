Advocacy

Training of Trainers for Media and Democracy Club Coordinators

The second batch of the Media and Information Literacy (MIL) Training of Trainers (ToT) for Media and Democracy Club (MDC) coordinators successfully concluded on 11th July 2025 in Trongsa. The TOT was attended by MDC Coordinators from Samtse Lower Secondary School in Samtse, Samcholing Middle Secondary School and Langthil Lower Secondary School in Trongsa, and Sakteng Lower Secondary School in Trashigang. Held from 7-11 July, the five-day training introduced participants to learner-centred facilitation approaches and hands-on activities in news writing, vlogging, photo story creation, and key media literacy concepts such as misinformation, disinformation, information disorder, and propaganda.

Youth Camp 2025

Youth Summit 2025 brought together 45 students aged 13 to 18 from across Bhutan. The programme began by introducing participants to the concept of being an 'active citizen, their rights and responsibilities, and the importance of their role in governance and development. Neebsh from Damphu MSS reflected, "Before today, I did not know what it meant to be an active citizen or that my rights and responsibilities were already recognised, especially through the UNCRC." Participants assessed the current state of child and youth participation in their schools and communities. Using participatory tools, they reflected on how their voices are acknowledged in decision-making and proposed child-centred recommendations. One participant called it "education beyond the classroom," valuing the experiential learning.

SCE TOT.

On 31st August 2025, Y-PEER Samtse successfully hosted its second Training of Trainers (ToT) at Samtse College of Education. The workshop highlighted crucial topics such as Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SHR), Young Key Populations (YKP), Gender-Based Violence (GBV), and youth-centered services.
